§6 — AI Non-Human Intelligence Findings

Finding 1: The Thursday Effect

Thursday: 801 violent calls — 35% more than Tuesday (607), 8% higher than Saturday (741). Thursday midnight is the single most violent hour-slot.

Implication: Violence doesn’t follow nightlife patterns. The Thursday spike suggests mid-week conflict escalation — economic stress or drug market rhythms.

Finding 2: Volume-Lethality Inversion

Western: fewest calls (14,435) but highest gun rate (0.72%). Southeastern: most calls (29,973) but lowest violence (1.4%). Correlation: r ≈ −0.65.

Implication: Low-call districts ≠ safe. Concentrated interpersonal violence, same pattern as DC’s District 7.

Finding 3: Bimodal Violence Clock

Two peaks: 2–4 PM and midnight. Most cities show single late-night peaks. Afternoon accounts for 12.3% of daily violence in 2 hours (8.3% expected).

Implication: Daylight violence at this scale = structural not opportunistic. Night-focused policing misses it.

Finding 4: The 5,841 Call Load

5,841 calls/day across 9 districts. Only 1.7% high-priority. Violent: 146/day citywide.

Implication: 78.9% non-emergency calls = massive resource dilution. 76K business checks compete with 795 gun events.

Finding 5: Eastern vs Central — Twin Ecosystems

Eastern: most guns (126), most shootings (12), few robberies (26). Central: most robberies (27), fewer guns (69).

EASTERN — ARMED TERRITORIAL
Gun-dominant, shooting-heavy. Violence is expressive — turf disputes. Midnight–4AM peak (40 gun calls).
CENTRAL — PREDATORY EXTRACTION
Robbery-dominant, lower gun rate. Violence is instrumental — victims of opportunity in downtown commercial zone.
