§8 — AI Non-Human Intelligence Findings

Finding 1: 88% Firearm Homicide Rate

376 of 428 homicides (87.9%) involved firearms. Combined with 949 carjackings and 5,264 robberies, Baltimore’s violent crime ecosystem is overwhelmingly firearms-driven. The handgun alone accounts for 139 deaths.

Finding 2: The June Killing Season

June generates 61 homicides — 2.6× September’s 23. Summer (May–Aug) accounts for 39.5% of annual homicides. This seasonal amplification is stronger than most US cities.

Finding 3: Volume-Lethality Inversion

Western: fewest 911 calls but highest gun rate (0.72%). Southeastern: most calls but lowest violence (1.4%). Correlation: r ≈ −0.65. Low-call districts = concentrated lethal violence.

Finding 4: Thursday Anomaly

Thursday: 804 violent calls — 35% more than Tuesday. Most violent single cell: Thursday midnight (43 calls). Baltimore’s violence defies the weekend pattern.

Finding 5: 2023 Auto Theft Explosion + Structural Violence

Auto theft tripled from 2,723 (2022) to 8,390 (2023) — a +208% surge, the largest single-category increase. Meanwhile carjackings dropped 27% (547→401). Structural violence zones persist: Penrose/Fayette (24.6%), Sandtown-Winchester (24.2%), Upton (23.7%) — 1 in 4 crimes is serious violent.
